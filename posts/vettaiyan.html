Film October 2024

Vettaiyan

Low expectations, reasonable outcome. Rajinikanth as a fallible hero is a genuinely interesting choice — just don't ask about the climax. Contains spoilers.

Given I did not enjoy the director's previous work Jai Bhim, and Rajinikanth's ideologies do not hold up well in modern times, I had very low expectations. But the movie manages to explore relevant social themes in an accessible, entertaining way.

Loved: Manju Warrier

While she is grossly under-utilised, even the few minutes she is on screen she has wonderful agency and charm. It is heartening to see an intentionally child-free woman who suffers no thugs (I loved the scene where Athiyan cuts the call from the thugs). She is of course brilliant in 'Manasilaayo' and is a beacon of hope for all 46-year-olds.

Did not like: Lost momentum in the 2nd half

The 1st half really sets things up for an interesting conversation about extrajudicial killings ("encounters"). Unfortunately the 2nd half does not leverage this well. By bringing in the education issue (which is a pertinent topic, of course), it loses focus and tries to do too much.

Loved: Rajini in an apron

It is a single scene but it was wonderful to see Rajinikanth in an apron making dosa for Saranya while Taara (Manju Warrier) is seated. Even in modern times, women are expected to be the one playing the host and preparing food — and to see a big star do this on screen made me so happy.

Did not like: The climax

Athiyan written as a fallible hero in the 1st half was refreshing and a welcome trend (following Maanadu). Not sure who is behind Rajinikanth choosing to do this role but it is highly influential that someone with such star power appear human on screen. Unfortunately in the end, Rajinikanth has no consequence for his actions, undercutting the overall message.

Loved: Malaysia Vasudevan

Until this movie, I have strongly held the view that they should stop bringing back artists alive through AI (think Rogue One, Alien: Romulus). But there was something perfect about seeing Rajini AI-voiced by Malaysia Vasudevan, with the help of his son Yugendran. I am not sure how I feel about the possibilities this is opening up for the industry but for this movie, it was an exciting choice.

Did not like: The flashback

While Vettaiyan isn't the violence porn that Jai Bhim was, there's still a violent rape scene which the movie anchors on and shows again, again, and again unnecessarily. It makes no sense that Saranya is calling Athiyan in Kanyakumari when she is in danger in Chennai, but it is shown as the pivotal drive for Rajini's character. Could have definitely been better.

Other points of note: Fahadh Fasil was great, even if his death was a bit cheesy. Ritika Singh's skills were under-utilised. Amitabh B was a formidable sparring partner who could have been leveraged a lot more intellectually in the 2nd half. Rana Daggubati was nothing more than eye-candy for the most part.

Was wonderful to see: a Muslim DSP; women in hijab dancing in the Manasilaayo song; a woman antagonist whose flaw is not that she is rich or educated.

Verdict

Better than I expected, worse than what it should be. Watch for Manju Warrier
