To Have and Have Not

1944
Film 1h 40m

For someone who was not bowled over by Casablanca (1942) unlike the rest of the western world, this movie absolutely landed. It has all the strengths of Casablanca - human stories in the backdrop of a war, a sense of melancholy for a past life, elaborate plot to outmanoeuver the autocratic government, music as an unnamed character. But it also has a charming wit which makes it a magic that Casablanca did not hold for me (I am aware this is ludicrous given the sheer existence of Ingrid Bergman). This movie is also elevated by a sensational Lauren Bacall with whom Humphrey Bogart (and the rest of humanity) can't help but fall in love.

The Unbearable Weight of Massive Talent

2022
Film 1h 45m

There's always a soft corner in me for movies that know exactly what they are and are unabashedly so. Nicholas Cage and Pedro Pascal are essentially being their real-life selves, and they are charming enough to pull this off. It gets maybe a little too self-referential in some places but overall, it is a well-crafted, underrated love letter to movies and the pleasure of sharing them with others.

The Addams Family

1991
Film 1h 39m

The best of the Addams Family franchise including the cartoon and recent Wednesday (which is nowhere close to the franchise spiritually - they are outcasts, not headed for Hogwarts) Raul Julia and Angelica Huston exude passion and devotion every time they are on screen. The movie has a whole lot of heart for something with a fairly straightforward plot. Comfortable, fun watch.

Legally Blonde

2001
Film 1h 36m

I almost passed on this movie because the setup felt like male fantasy (I was also maybe uptight about my own potential Ivy league education at that time) But now it is in my roster of comfort watches. Fun, clever in places, heartwarming throughout. Reese Witherspoon will of course go on to bring more roles that break the blonde stereotype in her career but this movie deserves a special place for the sheer number of unique women on screen: not all of them "good", not all of them perfect, all of them absolutely fun and human.

Hero

2002
Film 1h 39m

I've watched this about 20 times now. I've heard the argument about it being Chinese propaganda, and don't care. Every frame is poetry. Reunites Jet Li and Donnie Yen for their graceful combat, Tony Leung and Maggie Cheung so you'd believe in love again. The philosophy behind the colours is a wonderful rabbit hole for the curious. Emi Wada's costume design is a masterclass in seamless yet distinct wardrobe for a movie this visually rich. Tan Dun's score elevates, lingers, and haunts.
PSA: Do not watch the Director's extended cut - the theatrical cut is perfect.

Little Miss Sunshine

2006
Film 1h 41m

I watched a 76-year old woman, who is otherwise fairly conventional, not stop laughing for 5 solid minutes at the climax of this movie. Every character has incredible nuance, growth, and absurdity in a way that you can watch when you are going through a tragedy and still feel hopeful. Dysfunctional-but-well-intentioned-family as a genre is one that I think Dan Levy has since perfected but this might be one of the pioneers to get the dynamics right. Tight screenplay, incredible performances - one of those movies I wish I had watched when I was younger, just for the tear-jerking moments between Alan Arkin and Abigail Breslin.

Maanadu

2021
Film 2h 27m

A nepo project that I can get behind. The cast and crew is essentially Ilayaraja-Gangai Amaran lineage and friends and they seem to have had a lot of fun making this movie, which shows. STR in his most human role, SJ Suryah being used exactly to his strength, this is a self-aware science fiction that manages to not talk down to its audience while keeping Tamil cinema sensibilities. After Saroja, Venkat Prabhu seems to have swung the other way so much that his movies really step up when it has basically no female characters (Mankatha was a chore to get through even the first 15 minutes) - making this movie unmissable but bittersweet.

Lawrence of Arabia

1962
Film 3h 30m
"With Major Lawrence, mercy is a passion. With me, it is merely good manners. You may judge which motive is the more reliable."

Does not pass the Bechdel or cultural sensitivity tests, yet you cannot take your eyes off of this movie about the rise and downfall of a non-teenage chosen one. Gorgeous, well-paced, self-aware. For one of the longest movies ever made, it surprisingly never belabours its points. The dialogues and the London Philharmonic score stay with you. Recommend watching in one sitting.
