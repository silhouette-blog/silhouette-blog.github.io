To Have and Have Not

1944
Film 1h 40m

For someone who was not bowled over by Casablanca (1942) unlike the rest of the western world, this movie absolutely landed. It has the strengths of Casablanca plus a charming wit — and of course, a sensational Lauren Bacall with whom Humphrey Bogart (and the rest of humanity) can't help but fall in love.

The Unbearable Weight of Massive Talent

2022
Film 1h 45m

There's always a soft corner in me for movies that know exactly what they are and are unabashedly so. Nicholas Cage and Pedro Pascal are essentially being their real-life selves, and they are charming enough to pull this off. It gets maybe a little too self-referential in some places but overall, it is a well-crafted, underrated love letter to movies and the pleasure of sharing them with others.

Hero

2002
Film 1h 39m

I've watched this about 20 times now. I've heard the argument about it being Chinese propaganda, and don't care. Every frame is poetry. Reunites Jet Li and Donnie Yen for their graceful combat, Tony Leung and Maggie Cheung so you'd believe in love again. The philosophy behind the colours is a wonderful rabbit hole for the curious. Tan Dun's score elevates, lingers, and haunts.
PSA: Do not watch the Director's extended cut - the theatrical cut is perfect.

Lawrence of Arabia

1962
Film 3h 30m
"With Major Lawrence, mercy is a passion. With me, it is merely good manners. You may judge which motive is the more reliable."

Does not pass the Bechdel or cultural sensitivity tests, yet you cannot take your eyes off of this movie about the rise and downfall of a non-teenage chosen one. Gorgeous, well-paced, self-aware. For one of the longest movies ever made, it surprisingly never belabours its points. Recommend watching in one sitting.
